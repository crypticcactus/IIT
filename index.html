Project Idea

Overview

The Mekanik is a mobile phone application that will be both available across iPhone and Android that allows users to search a data base built around all makes and models of cars and list a variety of information. This information will range from oil grades to engine capacities and even common issues on any searched vehicle, allowing the average joe, an apprentice or even a qualified mechanic to have a world of resources available at their fingertips. The data base will be built off of all current known car knowledge and information readily available across the internet (forums, videos, manufactures sites ect) and will have the ability to grow and develop at later stages through user input and verification.

Motivation

This application will be useful to even the most inexperience car person as well as those that are qualified with years of experience. It allows the user to search the app for anything that relates to all car make and models, then from there branches off into multiple sub categories. This could be something as simple as tyre pressure, oil grade for your car or even something more experienced such as changing the transmission fluid. What stands out about this app is its ability to have everything that one would need to know about any car make and model, viewed in simplicity and on their smart device. Removing the need for countless hours of forum surfing, lengthy videos or information that only qualified mechanics know can be readily available and accessed by anyone who downloads the app. At present I was unable to find any app of similar function making this a project that could prove very useful to millions of people.

Detailed Description

Mekanik will feature a simple designed UI that from the get go allows users to navigate with ease to their desired end search result what ever that may be. Upon downloading the app and opening it for the first time users will be prompted to enter their age as well as their experience within the mechanic scene allowing us to gather information and use data analysis to help develop an app that is able to meet the requirements of the users. The home screen will feature a Basic UI with the home screen having a few quick links to common search results that are more likely going to be searched. Things like oil grades, oil changes, tyre pressure and even jump starting a car. There will be a search function button on the home screen that upon input will move to a new screen where there will be a list of categories that can be filled in to reach the desired search result. The user will be able to search based off as little or as much as they want and have the ability to create their own search through the use of keywords if they know exactly what they are looking for. On this page we expect to have Model, Make, Year and Keywords. This will cover every make and model that is known to exist. So whether it be information on a 2009 Ford Falcon FG or a 1967 Chevy Impala hardtop powered by a 502-cubic-inch big-block, you can find it. From the users input it will then bring up another page with a list of sub categories from which the user can now begin to navigate to a finer search result. This page will contain such things as Transmission, Engine, Fuel Type, Oil, Induction/Turbo, Colour ect. Clicking on any of these will then bring up the last of the sub categories before providing the user with all information in regards to the topic. For example clicking on Oil will bring up a summary of oil related information - oil type, oil grade, oil consumption ect and scrolling down will display variety of articles and videos on the topic of oil which have all been gathered and implemented into our database. A Few simple clicks and the user will have access to things that may have taken them a ages to find online depending on the complexity of the task. A navigation bar on the left hand side will slide across and give the ability for a user to sign in/join (completely optional), and will allow those that have to save searches and customise their home screen to feature information they find relatable and use on a regular basis. There will also be a Home button that will take the user back to their home screen as well as notifications for any topic that has been saved. This will range from new information, to changes on existing information and anything else that correlates to that said saved search result. In later versions we expect to add a report feature for broken links, wrong data or otherwise not listed information which at this stage will be a function on the home screen, but could easily be something that is accessible even when the user is a few searches in. As as we continue to tweak, modify and update The Mekanik, users will be allowed to submit information across any car or topic (pending cross checking and data verification) giving the application the ability to grow and develop as more information becomes available. Our intended end goal is to have an application that contains all possible information that any one individual could need, and have so in the palm of their hand. The database will be built by information that is available online, be it forums, manufactures websites or even car sales and then finally by working with mechanics on the more complex and detailed tasks.
